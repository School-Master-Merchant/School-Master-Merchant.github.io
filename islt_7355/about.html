    I have been working as a teacher for over a decade. For the past three years, I have been teaching k-5th grade elementary music. I also support the English as a second language students for all the grades k-12! It is a small community, so I only have a few ESL students. I used to also teach Spanish and Latin. As a teacher during these past few years, I have realized how technology has impacted every industry; including education. Currently, teacher lesson plans are now accessible online, and for a moment in time, teachers had to learn how to create lessons that were digitally accessed. The type of instructional design that I have prepared is from what I have created in my own classroom or for my TPT store. I believe my foundation and experience in teaching will be advantageous as an instructional designer, but I know I have a lot to learn!
    As a graduate student in the Learning Technologies and Design program, I am hoping to learn skills that go beyond subject matter & instructional-pedagogy procedures, so that I can learn the technology necessary in our current times. I also like the idea of the lateral mobility for instructional designers that give opportunities to work for schools, universities, or business-industries.
